Call Page Redesign - 5 Layout Options

1
Compact Dialer + Customer Feed Below

Key Changes: Reduces the dialer size by making the number pad collapsible (show/hide). Adds a Customer Feed panel directly below the dialer showing customer activity, notes history, and contact details. Session Stats stays in the queue panel but is collapsed by default with a click-to-expand header.

Pros

  • Minimal change from current layout
  • Customer info is front and center
  • Dial pad can be hidden when not needed
  • Easy to implement

Cons

  • Center column can get long/scrolly
  • Customer feed competes for space with dialer

2
Customer-First Layout (Stacked Right)

Key Changes: Prioritizes the customer profile by giving it a full center column. Moves the dialer to a smaller left column combined with the queue. AI Coach and Transcript are stacked vertically on the right instead of side-by-side. Session stats becomes a collapsible bar at the top of the queue.

Pros

  • Customer info gets prime real estate
  • More vertical space for AI Coach and Transcript
  • Queue + Dialer grouped logically

Cons

  • AI Coach and Transcript narrower
  • Bigger layout change to implement
  • Queue column might feel cramped

3
Floating Dialer (Minimal Footprint)

Key Changes: The dialer becomes a small floating widget in the bottom-right corner that stays visible during calls. This frees up a full column for the Customer Profile. Session stats appears as a collapsible top bar. Queue stays on the left. AI Coach and Transcript get more horizontal space.

Pros

  • Maximum space for customer & AI content
  • Dialer always accessible but not intrusive
  • Modern, app-like feel
  • Clean two-column main area

Cons

  • Floating element can obscure content
  • May feel unfamiliar to users
  • Dial pad requires expanding/clicking

4
Tabbed Customer Panel

Key Changes: Customer info gets a dedicated center column with tabs (Profile | Activity | Notes | History). The dialer is compacted and placed at the top of the queue column. Session stats is inline with queue header. AI Coach and Transcript split the right area.

Pros

  • Organized customer information
  • Tabs reduce visual clutter
  • Each info type has dedicated space
  • Logical information hierarchy

Cons

  • Requires clicking tabs to see info
  • Can't see all customer info at once
  • More clicks to access information

5
Dashboard Grid Layout

Key Changes: Complete restructure into a dashboard-style grid. Top row has compact dialer, session stats, and quick actions. Bottom left has the queue + customer profile. Bottom right has AI Coach and Transcript side by side. Everything is visible at a glance.

Pros

  • Everything visible at once
  • Session stats always visible (not hidden)
  • Dashboard feel - modern and clean
  • Clear visual hierarchy

Cons

  • Biggest change from current layout
  • Less vertical space for each panel
  • May feel information-dense
  • Queue gets less dedicated space

Summary Comparison

Design Dialer Customer Info Session Stats Best For
1. Compact + Feed Below Collapsible dial pad Feed below dialer In queue (collapsed) Minimal change, quick win
2. Customer-First Mini in queue column Full center column Collapsible top bar Customer-centric workflows
3. Floating Dialer Floating widget Top bar + expandable In queue header Maximum content space
4. Tabbed Panel Compact above queue Tabbed center panel In queue header Organized, lots of info
5. Dashboard Grid Top row compact Bottom left panel Always visible top row Dashboard lovers, metrics focus

My Recommendation

Design 1 is the safest and quickest to implement - it keeps the familiar layout while addressing your main concerns (smaller dialer, customer feed below, collapsible stats).

Design 4 (Tabbed) or Design 5 (Dashboard) would be more impactful but require more development effort. Design 5 gives the best "at a glance" experience.

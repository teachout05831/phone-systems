Follow-Up Sequence

Key Stat: 60% of replies come after the first follow-up!

Recommended 5-Email Sequence

Email #1
Day 1

Initial Outreach

Introduce value proposition. Personalized hook about them, problem you solve, what you offer, clear CTA.

Email #2
Days 3-4

Quick Reminder

Short bump of previous email. "Just wanted to bump this to the top of your inbox. Did you get a chance to look at [previous topic]?"

Email #3
Days 7-10

New Angle

Different value prop or insight. "I was thinking about [their specific situation] and wanted to share [new insight/resource/angle]."

Email #4
Days 14-17

Social Proof

Case study or testimonial. "[Company similar to them] was in a similar situation and [specific result they got]."

Email #5
Days 21-30

Breakup Email

Create urgency, last chance. "I don't want to keep filling up your inbox, so this will be my last email. If [problem] isn't a priority right now, totally understand."

Follow-Up Tips

  • Keep follow-ups shorter than initial email
  • Reference previous email ("Wanted to follow up on...")
  • Add new value or angle each time
  • Breakup email often gets highest response rate
  • Don't be needy - provide value

Multi-Channel Sequence Example

Day 1: Email - Initial outreach
Day 2: LinkedIn - View profile
Day 3: LinkedIn - Connect request
Day 5: Email - Follow-up #1
Day 7: LinkedIn - Message (if connected)
Day 10: Email - Follow-up #2 (new angle)
Day 14: Call - If no response
Day 17: Email - Social proof
Day 21: Email + LinkedIn - Final message

Channel-Specific Tips

LinkedIn

  • - Warm up by engaging with their content first
  • - Keep connection requests short (< 300 chars)
  • - Personalize based on profile/content
  • - Don't pitch in connection request

Cold Calling

  • - Best after email opens/engagement
  • - Morning calls (9-11 AM) often better
  • - Have a script but sound natural
  • - Voicemail should be < 30 seconds

Red Flags to Avoid

Don't Do This

  • "I hope this email finds you well" - Overused, shows no effort
  • Attach files in cold email - Hurts deliverability
  • Multiple CTAs - Confuses recipient
  • Long paragraphs - Won't get read
  • Talking about yourself first - They don't care yet
  • Being pushy - Creates resistance
  • Sending too frequently - Annoying
  • Generic templates - Low response

Metrics to Track

Open Rate
Benchmark: 40-60%
If below: Fix subject line, sender name
Reply Rate
Benchmark: 4-8%
If below: Fix email content, targeting
Bounce Rate
Benchmark: < 2%
If above: Clean your list
Unsubscribe Rate
Benchmark: < 0.5%
If above: Check targeting, frequency
Spam Complaints
Benchmark: < 0.1%
If above: Major issue - stop and fix

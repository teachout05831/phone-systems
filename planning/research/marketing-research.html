Marketing & Paid Ads Research

Ad Platform APIs, Attribution Models, and Dashboard Best Practices

Research Overview

This document covers research on integrating paid advertising platforms into the Outreach System. The goal is to create a unified marketing command center that pulls data from multiple ad platforms, tracks lead attribution, and provides cross-channel analytics.

Key Insight: What to Build vs. What NOT to Build

  • BUILD: Unified lead database, source attribution, cross-platform dashboard, CPL calculator, ROAS tracking
  • DON'T BUILD: Ad creation, audience management, bid optimization, creative testing - use native platforms for these

Attribution Models Research

Attribution determines how credit for conversions is assigned to different marketing touchpoints. 75% of marketers now use multi-channel attribution according to industry surveys.

First-Touch Attribution

100% credit to the first interaction that brought the lead. Simple to implement, good for understanding awareness channels.

Best for: Top-of-funnel analysis, brand awareness campaigns

Last-Touch Attribution

100% credit to the final interaction before conversion. Default in most platforms, easy to understand.

Best for: Direct response campaigns, simple funnels

Linear Attribution

Equal credit to all touchpoints in the customer journey. Fair but may undervalue key moments.

Best for: Long sales cycles, multiple touchpoint journeys

Time-Decay Attribution

More credit to touchpoints closer to conversion. Recognizes recency importance.

Best for: Short consideration cycles, promotional campaigns

Position-Based (U-Shaped)

40% to first touch, 40% to last touch, 20% split among middle interactions.

Best for: Lead generation focus, values both discovery and conversion

Data-Driven Attribution

Machine learning assigns credit based on historical data patterns. Requires 15,000+ conversions over 90 days.

Best for: Advanced teams with high conversion volume

Recommendation: Start Simple

  • Begin with First-Touch attribution - it's easiest to implement and explains where leads originate
  • Add Last-Touch as secondary view to understand what closes deals
  • Move to Position-Based once you have 6+ months of data
  • Only consider Data-Driven if you have 500+ conversions/month

Recommended Implementation Approach

1. Data Architecture

Pull data via APIs on a schedule (hourly for active campaigns, daily for historical). Store in Supabase with proper indexing for fast queries. Don't try to manage ads from the system - just view and analyze.

2. Sync Strategy

Use webhooks where available (FB Lead Forms, LinkedIn Lead Gen). For metrics, batch pull during off-peak hours. Cache aggressively - ad metrics don't change retroactively.

3. Lead Matching

Match incoming leads to ad data using UTM parameters, click IDs (fbclid, gclid), and email matching. Build deduplication logic to prevent counting same lead twice across platforms.

4. Platform Priority

Phase 1: Facebook + Google (covers 80%+ of ad spend typically)
Phase 2: TikTok + LinkedIn (add based on actual usage)
Phase 3: Custom webhooks for other sources
